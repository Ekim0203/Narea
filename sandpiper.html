Spoon-billed sandpiper

class I

(Scientific Name) Calidris pygmaea

Body length: 14-16 cm

Breeding grounds: Russian Far East
Migration route: Stops in East Asia, including South Korea,
before wintering in Southeast Asia (Myanmar, Bangladesh)

Critically Endangered
Population has declined drastically due to habitat loss and poaching,
with fewer than 500 individuals remaining in the wild

Connection to Korea
South Korea’s west and south coasts serve as important stopover sites
International conservation efforts are crucial for its survival

  • Scientific Name Calidris pygmaeaitem
  • Korean Name 넓적부리도요
  • English Name Spoon-billed Sandpiper
