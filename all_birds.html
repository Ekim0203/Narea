Birds overview


↑

Big Sandpiper

Habitat: Wetlands

Large migratory shorebird that relies on clean, undisturbed mudflats.

Radde’s Warbler

Habitat: Forests

Inhabits dense forest undergrowth and is sensitive to deforestation.

Common Kingfisher

Habitat: Rivers & Streams

A vibrant bird known for diving to catch small fish in clean rivers.

Black-faced Spoonbill

Habitat: Wetlands & Estuaries

Rare spoon-billed wader relying on tidal flats for feeding and nesting.

Steller's Sea Eagle

Habitat: Rivers & Coastal Areas

One of the largest raptors, visiting Korea in winter. Powerful and majestic.

Eurasian Eagle-Owl

Habitat: Forests & Cliffs

A powerful nocturnal hunter with orange eyes and tufts. Lives deep in forested cliffs.

Fairy Pitta

Habitat: Moist Forests

A rare summer visitor with vivid multicolored feathers. Breeds in quiet wooded areas.

Great Bustard

Habitat: Grasslands, open fields

Large and powerful, the Great Bustard is one of the heaviest flying birds, known for its striking plumage and elaborate courtship displays.

Eurasian hobby

Habitat: Grasslands, open areas, forests

Small and agile, the Eurasian Hobby is known for its speed and precision in hunting insects and small birds in flight. It is a skilled predator with impressive aerial acrobatics.

Chinese Crested Tern

Habitat: Coastal Islands

Extremely rare seabird with a spiky crest and yellow-tipped black bill.
